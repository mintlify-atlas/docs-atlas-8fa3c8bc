## Overview

The `seo-audit` skill provides an on-page SEO checklist and keyword research workflow for blog posts. Load this skill before publishing to verify SEO elements.

**Skill file**: `~/workspace/source/skills/seo-audit.md`

**Trigger**: When auditing a blog post for SEO, doing keyword research, or checking on-page optimization

## On-Page SEO Checklist

Run through this checklist for every post before publishing:

### Title & Meta

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Title Tag" icon="heading">
    - **Length**: Under 60 characters (55-60 is ideal)
    - **Keyword**: Includes primary keyword naturally
    - **Compelling**: Benefit-driven or question-based
    
    **Example**: "Why Your Laptop Is Killing Your AI Automation" (54 chars)
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Meta Description" icon="file-lines">
    - **Length**: 150-160 characters
    - **Keyword**: Includes primary keyword
    - **CTA**: Has a call to action
    
    **Example**: "Running AI agents on your laptop? Here's why dedicated hardware saves you time and money. Learn the business case for always-on automation." (158 chars)
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="URL Slug" icon="link">
    - **Short**: 3-5 words max
    - **Keyword-rich**: Includes primary keyword
    - **Hyphenated**: Use hyphens, not underscores
    
    **Example**: `/laptop-ai-agent-problems` or `/dedicated-hardware-ai`
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

### Content Structure

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="H1 Tag" icon="h1">
    - **One per page** (never multiple H1s)
    - **Matches title intent** (can differ slightly for readability)
    - **Includes primary keyword** naturally
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="H2 Tags" icon="h2">
    - **4-8 per post** for 2,000-3,000 word content
    - **Include secondary keywords** naturally
    - Examples: "The Problem", "The Solution", "Real-World Example", "Getting Started", "FAQ"
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="H3 Tags" icon="h3">
    - Used for **subsections under H2s**
    - Never skip heading levels (don't jump from H2 to H4)
    - Can include long-tail keyword variants
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="First 100 Words" icon="paragraph">
    Primary keyword must appear **naturally** in the opening paragraphs.
    
    **Why**: Search engines weight early content more heavily. Readers confirm they're in the right place.
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Word Count" icon="file-word">
    - **Target**: 2,000-3,000 words
    - **Why**: Long-form content ranks better for informational queries
    - **Note**: Quality over quantity — don't pad for word count
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

### Keywords

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Primary Keyword" icon="key">
    Should appear in:
    - Title tag
    - H1
    - First paragraph (ideally first 100 words)
    - 1-2 H2 headings
    - Conclusion
    
    **Example**: If primary keyword is "local AI agent", it should appear ~8-12 times in a 2,500-word post.
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Keyword Density" icon="percent">
    - **Target**: 1-2% of total words
    - **Test**: Search (Cmd/Ctrl+F) for your keyword — does it feel natural?
    - **Avoid**: Keyword stuffing (using keyword unnaturally or excessively)
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Secondary Keywords" icon="tags">
    - **2-3 related terms** woven throughout
    - Examples: If primary is "AI agent", secondary might be "AI assistant", "automation tool", "AI workflow"
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="LSI Keywords" icon="layer-group">
    **Latent Semantic Indexing** keywords = related phrases and synonyms
    
    **Example**: For "AI agent", LSI keywords include:
    - AI automation
    - Intelligent assistant
    - AI-powered workflow
    - Autonomous agent
    - AI tool
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

### Links

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Internal Links" icon="link">
    - **Minimum**: Link to 2-3 other posts on openclaws.blog
    - **Placement**: Natural within body text, not forced
    - **Anchor text**: Descriptive (not "click here")
    
    **Example**: "Learn how to [set up OpenClaw on Ubuntu](/setup-openclaw-ubuntu) in under 10 minutes."
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="External Links" icon="arrow-up-right-from-square">
    - **Minimum**: 1-2 authoritative sources
    - **Purpose**: Cite data, reference documentation, link to reputable sites
    - **Examples**: Documentation sites, research papers, GitHub repos, industry reports
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Anchor Text" icon="text">
    - **Descriptive**: Use the target keyword or a natural phrase
    - **Avoid**: "click here", "this link", "read more" (non-descriptive)
    
    ✅ Good: "Read our [comparison of local AI assistants](/local-ai-comparison)"
    ❌ Bad: "Click [here](/local-ai-comparison) to read more"
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Broken Links" icon="link-slash">
    Before publishing, verify all links work:
    ```bash
    # Check for 404s (if you have a link checker installed)
    wget --spider -r -nd -nv -l 1 https://openclaws.blog/YOUR-POST-SLUG 2>&1 | grep -B 2 '404'
    ```
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

### Media

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Images" icon="image">
    - **Minimum**: 1-2 relevant images or diagrams per post
    - **Purpose**: Break up text, illustrate concepts, improve engagement
    - **Format**: WebP or PNG/JPG (optimized for web)
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Alt Text" icon="closed-captioning">
    Every image needs descriptive alt text:
    - **Describe the image** for screen readers
    - **Include keyword** where natural (not forced)
    
    **Example**: `<img src="mac-mini-ai-setup.jpg" alt="Mac Mini connected to monitor running OpenClaw AI agent">`
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="File Names" icon="file-image">
    Use descriptive file names, not generic ones:
    
    ✅ Good: `local-ai-agent-architecture.png`
    ❌ Bad: `screenshot-1.png`, `IMG_1234.jpg`
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

### Readability

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Short Paragraphs" icon="paragraph">
    - **Target**: 2-4 sentences max per paragraph
    - **Why**: Easier to scan on mobile devices
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Scannable" icon="list">
    Make content easy to skim:
    - **Bold** key terms
    - **Lists** for features and steps
    - **Tables** for comparisons
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Comparison Table" icon="table">
    At least **one comparison table** per post:
    - 3+ columns, 5+ rows
    - Structured data that search engines can parse
    - Makes complex info scannable
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="FAQ Section" icon="question">
    3-6 questions targeting "People Also Ask" queries:
    - Use natural language (how people ask AI assistants)
    - 50-150 words per answer
    - Address common objections
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Keyword Research Workflow

### 1. Find What People Are Asking

Use CLI tools to discover trending topics and questions:

```bash
# Scan Reddit for common questions
au reddit search "how to [topic]" top month 10
au reddit search "[topic] vs" top month 10
au reddit search "[topic] best" top month 10

# Check Hacker News for trending discussions
au news latest --provider hacker-news --limit 15

# Look at YouTube for popular tutorial topics
au youtube transcript "https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=VIDEO_ID"
```

See `~/workspace/source/skills/research.md` for more research commands.

### 2. Analyze Search Intent

For each keyword, identify the user's intent:

| Intent | Signal | Content Type |
|--------|--------|-------------|
| **Informational** | "what is", "how to", "guide" | Blog post, tutorial |
| **Comparison** | "vs", "best", "alternative" | Comparison post with table |
| **Transactional** | "download", "install", "setup" | Tutorial with CTA |
| **Navigational** | Brand name, product name | Landing page or docs |

**Example**: "local AI agent setup" is **transactional** (user wants to install something) → write a tutorial with step-by-step setup.

### 3. Evaluate Keyword Difficulty

No paid SEO tools needed — use these proxies:

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Reddit Engagement" icon="reddit">
    High upvotes/comments = validated demand. If a Reddit thread about the topic has 500+ upvotes, there's real interest.
  </Card>
  <Card title="HN Discussion" icon="y-combinator">
    Front page = strong interest. Topics that hit HN front page are worth writing about.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Existing Content Quality" icon="magnifying-glass">
    Google the keyword. Can we write something significantly better than what's ranking?
  </Card>
  <Card title="Long-Tail Opportunity" icon="arrow-down">
    More specific = easier to rank. "local ai agent mac setup" is easier than "ai agent".
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

### 4. Build a Keyword Cluster

For each blog post, define:

- **Primary keyword** — Main topic (1 phrase)
  - Example: "dedicated hardware for AI agents"
- **Secondary keywords** — 2-3 related terms
  - Examples: "AI agent hosting", "always-on automation", "Mac Mini AI setup"
- **Long-tail variants** — 3-5 specific phrases
  - Examples: "why use dedicated hardware for AI", "best hardware for local AI agents", "Mac Mini vs laptop for AI agents"
- **Questions to answer** — 3-6 "People Also Ask" style questions for FAQ
  - Examples: "Can I run AI agents on my laptop?", "How much does dedicated hardware cost?", "Is Mac Mini good for AI agents?"

## Post-Publish SEO Check

After publishing, verify SEO elements are live:

```bash
# Get the published post
wp post get <post-id> --field=post_content --ssh="$WP_SSH"

# Check title and slug
wp post get <post-id> --fields=post_title,post_name,post_status --ssh="$WP_SSH"
```

**Manual checks**:

1. **Visit the live URL** — does it render correctly?
2. **View page source** (right-click → View Page Source) — is the title tag and meta description correct?
3. **Internal links** — do 2-3 related existing posts link back to this new post?
4. **Update the content calendar** — mark as published in `content/calendar.md`

## AEO (Answer Engine Optimization)

Modern SEO includes optimizing for AI answer engines (ChatGPT, Perplexity, Google AI Overviews).

**Key differences from traditional SEO**:

| Traditional SEO | Answer Engine Optimization (AEO) |
|-----------------|----------------------------------|
| Optimize for ranking position | Optimize for being quoted/cited |
| Target Google search | Target ChatGPT, Perplexity, Claude |
| Keyword density matters | Clear definitions and Q&A patterns matter |
| Backlinks are critical | First-party data and expertise are critical |

## Real Example

Here's how the skill guides an SEO audit:

**User request**: "Audit the latest blog post for SEO before publishing."

**Warden's workflow**:
1. Load `seo-audit` skill → understand checklist items
2. Read the draft post content
3. Check title tag: "Why Your Laptop Is Killing Your AI Automation" — 54 chars ✅, includes primary keyword ✅
4. Check meta description: Present? 150-160 chars? Includes keyword? Has CTA?
5. Check URL slug: `/laptop-ai-automation-problems` — short ✅, hyphenated ✅, keyword-rich ✅
6. Check H1: Matches title intent? Includes keyword?
7. Check H2s: 4-8 present? Include secondary keywords?
8. Check first 100 words: Does primary keyword appear naturally?
9. Check internal links: Found 3 links to other posts ✅
10. Check external links: Found 2 citations (GitHub, research paper) ✅
11. Check images: 2 images, both have descriptive alt text ✅
12. Check FAQ section: 5 Q&A pairs ✅
13. Report findings: "SEO audit complete. All checklist items passed. Ready to publish."
